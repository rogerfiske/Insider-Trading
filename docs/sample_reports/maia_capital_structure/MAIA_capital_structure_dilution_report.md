## Hidden / Lagged Institutional Ownership Discussion

**Key Mechanisms That May Obscure Institutional Positions**:

### 1. Beneficial Ownership Blockers
The March 2026 424B5 mentions "Minimum Price" compliance under NYSE rules and references prior offerings with beneficial ownership limitations. Typical blockers:
- **4.99% blocker**: Holder cannot exercise warrants/options if it would result in beneficial ownership exceeding 4.99%
- **9.99% blocker**: Same concept at 9.99% threshold
- **Purpose**: Allows investors to take large positions without triggering 13D/13G filing requirements (which apply at 5% beneficial ownership)
- **Effect**: Large institutional investors may hold significant economic exposure without appearing in 13D/13G filings

### 2. Pre-Funded Warrants (Not Present in March 2026 Offering)
- **How they work**: Warrants with exercise price of $0.0001, economically equivalent to common stock
- **Why used**: Allow investors to take large positions with minimal capital outlay while staying below beneficial ownership thresholds until exercise
- **Visibility**: Pre-funded warrants are disclosed in offering documents but may not be exercised immediately, creating lag between economic exposure and disclosed ownership
- **Note**: The March 2026 public offering did NOT include pre-funded warrants, but prior MAIA offerings may have used them

### 3. 13F Reporting Lag and Thresholds
- **Quarterly lag**: 13F filings have 45-day lag after quarter-end
- **Threshold**: Only managers with $100M+ AUM must file
- **Exclusions**: Positions <10,000 shares or <$200,000 may be omitted
- **Effect**: Institutional positions may not be visible for 4-5 months after initial purchase

### 4. 13D/13G Reporting Thresholds
- **5% trigger**: Only beneficial owners of 5%+ must file
- **Aggregation**: Related entities may need to aggregate holdings
- **Effect**: Beneficial ownership blockers at 4.99% keep investors just below the disclosure threshold

### 5. Private Placement / PIPE Visibility
The December 2025 private placement to "accredited investors including a Company director" was a private offering:
- **Limited disclosure**: Investor names not publicly disclosed unless they are insiders
- **Warrants attached**: Each share came with a warrant at $1.36 exercise price
- **Economic exposure**: Investors have exposure through both shares and warrants, but only the shares count toward beneficial ownership until warrant exercise
- **Effect**: Large investors may have significant economic exposure with limited public visibility

### 6. Form 144 as Indicator of Future Sales
- **Purpose**: Form 144 is filed when insiders or affiliates plan to sell restricted stock
- **Lead time**: Typically filed shortly before sale
- **MAIA Status**: No Form 144 filings found, suggesting no imminent insider sales
- **Limitation**: Form 144 only covers restricted securities and affiliate sales, not open market sales by non-affiliates

### 7. Unnamed "Healthcare-Dedicated Investors"
The original CP23A report referenced "healthcare-dedicated investors" language, likely from prior offering documents. This phrasing suggests:
- **Specialized investors**: Likely institutional healthcare/biotech-focused funds
